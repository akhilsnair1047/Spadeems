Sonam Wangchuk: The Inspiration From Ladakh There are people who work for themselves, and there are people whose work inspires others and makes a difference in society. One such innovator is *Sonam Wangchuk*, the man behind countless innovations and initiatives. Sonam Wangchuk is an Indian engineer, innovator, and education reformist. He is the founding director of the Students’ Educational and Cultural Movement of Ladakh, which was founded in 1988 by a group of students who had been in his own words, the ‘victims’ of an alien education system foisted on them. This is the man whose extraordinary life had inspired the filmmaker of ‘3 idiots’ to craft the unforgettable character of Phunsukh Wangdu. Later, his ground-breaking innovations like ‘Ice Stupas’ and solar heated mud huts put him on the global radar for finding sustainable solutions on difficult terrains. His experience and wisdom have been used by several governmental agencies, in the capacity of an advisor and consultant. He was entrusted with the task of formulating the policy on Education and Tourism as part of the Vision Document Ladakh 2025.
